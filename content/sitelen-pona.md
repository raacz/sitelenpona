# What does Sitelen Pona look like?

In Sitelen Pona, each Toki Pona word can be represented by one simple pictogram or, ‘glyph.’ Sitelen Pona’s glyphs often look like the things they describe. For example, here are the symbols for tomo (house, structure), pipi (bug, insect), and sike (circle, round, cycle).

# sitelen pona li seme tawa lukin

sitelen pona la nimi wan li sitelen wan. tenpo mute la sitelen li lukin sama toki ona. o lukin e sitelen anpa:

![glyphs for tomo, pipi and sike](/assets/sitelen/tomo-pipi-sike.svg)


# What does Sitelen Pona look like?

Sitelen Pona is usually written from left to right, from top to bottom. Glyphs can be combined. To say a bug’s house, tomo pipi, we might place the pipi glyph into the empty space in the tomo glyph. To talk about a circular bug or pipi sike, like ladybug, we might put the sike glyph above the pipi glyph.

# sitelen pona li seme tawa lukin

nasin tawa pi sitelen pona li sama sitelen [la.sina:]. sitelen mute li ken kama wan. tomo pipi la pipi li ken lon insa pi sitelen tomo. pipi sike la sitelen sike li ken lon sewi pi pipi.

![combined glyphs: pipi inside tomo, sike above pipi](/assets/sitelen/tomopipi-pipisike.svg)

# What are the advantages of Sitelen Pona? 

For Toki Pona, a language that has less than 150 words, writing with pictographs in this way feels so natural! It can save so much space and time when writing by hand. Because it’s such a visual script, some find text written with Sitelen Pona easier to understand than text in the Latin alphabet.
